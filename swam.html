🏛 swamimalai TEMPLE

A b o u t :

The temple is said to have been built in early 2nd century BC by Parantaka Chola I. It is the fourth of six Aarupadaiveedu (the six most significant shrines dedicated to Murugan in Tamil Nadu) and is important because this is when and where the secret of the Pranava Mantra “Aum” was revealed.

Location:10, Vatampokki Street, Taluk, Swamimalai, Kumbakonam, Tamil Nadu 612302

Main god: Lord Murugan

The temple is open from 6.00 a.m. to 12.00 p.m. and from 4.00 p.m. to 9.00 p.m.

Significance:

It is one of the Aarupadai veedu

According to hindu belief swamimalai is the place where Lord Muruga preached pranava mantara to his own father
